Welcome to the SafelySolo beta! We're building a tool that helps you stay safe while adventuring alone—whether it's a sunrise hike, a late-night run, or just a solo walk to your car. This guide will help you get the most out of the app.

Beta Note: SafelySolo is currently in active development. While core safety features are functional, you may encounter minor bugs. Please do not rely on SafelySolo as your only safety measure during the beta period.

1How SafelySolo works

SafelySolo is built on a simple "dead man's switch" principle:

  • You set a timer for your activity.
  • If you reach your destination or finish your task, you Check In to stop the timer.
  • If the timer expires before you check in, the app automatically sends an SMS to your trusted contacts with your last known location.

2Initial setup

To function correctly, SafelySolo needs two key permissions:

Location Services

Set this to "Always Allow" (or "While Using" on some versions). The app needs to know your location at the moment a timer expires so it can tell your contacts where you are. We do not track you when a timer isn't running.

Notifications

Enable notifications so SafelySolo can warn you when your timer is about to expire. This gives you a chance to check in or extend the timer before an alert is sent.

3Setting your first timer

When you start a solo activity:

  1. Choose a duration that covers your expected activity plus a small buffer (e.g., if a hike takes 60 minutes, set the timer for 75).
  2. Tap Start Timer.
  3. You can lock your phone and go. The app runs in the background.
  4. When you're safe, open the app and tap Check In.

4Managing trusted contacts

Go to the Contacts tab to add people you trust.

  • We recommend adding 2–3 people who are likely to be near their phones.
  • Pro Tip: Use the "Send Test Alert" feature to let your contacts know what a SafelySolo text looks like. This prevents unnecessary panic when they see a real alert.

5Home Screen Widgets

SafelySolo supports iOS and Android widgets so you can start or stop a timer without digging for the app icon.

  • Small Widget: Shows your current status or the time remaining on an active check-in.
  • Medium Widget: Provides quick-start buttons for common timer lengths (e.g., 15m, 30m, 1h).
  • To add: Long-press your home screen, tap the (+) or "Widgets" button, and search for SafelySolo.

6Siri & Voice Support

Powered by App Intents, SafelySolo allows you to manage your safety hands-free—perfect for when you're already on the move or wearing gloves.

  • "Siri, start a 30-minute check-in with SafelySolo."
  • "Siri, check in with SafelySolo." (Stops the active timer)
  • "Siri, add 15 minutes to my SafelySolo timer."

On Android, you can use similar commands with Google Assistant to trigger these actions.

7Tips & Tricks

  • Extend the clock: If your hike is taking longer than expected, you can tap "Add 15 Minutes" to your active timer without having to stop and restart it.
  • Low Battery Mode: SafelySolo is optimized for battery life, but keep an eye on your levels during long activities. If your phone dies, the app cannot send an alert at the expiration time (alerts are triggered by the server if the check-in is missed, provided the phone had signal when the timer was started).
  • Signal Check: Ensure you have a cellular connection when you start the timer so the SafelySolo server knows you're out there.
